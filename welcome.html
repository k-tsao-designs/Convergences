About this Project

Navigation

This is an experimental storytelling website that tells two stories at once by taking advantage of the dynamic and nonlinear reading format available in the digital medium, as opposed to a static and linear book. Scroll down to experience the story sequence, and explore the words and images to find links to the next step in the story or to a corresponding convergence point in a different story.

In this version of the experience, you'll wander through the stories East of the Sun, West of the Moon from Norway and The Singing, Springing Lark from Germany. They both fall into the category of “The Animal Groom” fairytales, and you'll soon find many repetitions and similarities no matter which paths you follow.

Stories

Mythology, folktales, and fairytales are an ideal genre with which to explore these convergence points in similar stories. Since they come from an oral tradition, every time an individual participates in the tradition by retelling the story, the possibility opens of recombining common story elements, such as the taboo, the quest, the helpful stranger, and so on, in different ways by forgetting how one story goes and adding elements of another.

By updating the storytelling tradition to today's world, I invite you to explore these fairytale elements and stitch together your own tale out of these folklore fragments.
